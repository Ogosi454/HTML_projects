The life history of Sir Tim Berners-Lee

Sir Tim Berners-Lee is a prominent computer scientist best known as the Inventor of the World Wide Web.

He was born in London - 1995, and graduated from the University of Oxford with a degree in physics in 1976. While working as a software engineering consultant at CERN, the European particle physics labaratory in Geneva, he drew up a proposal in 1989for a global hypertext document system to help researchers share information efficiently.

Between 1990and 1991, he wrote the software for the first web server and the first web client, or browser. Crucially he decided to make the underlying code and protocols (HTML, URL, and HTTP) available on a royalty-free basis, which allowed the web to grow expotentially without central control.

In 1994, Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology to develop open standards for the Web's continued growth and ensure it works well for everyone. His contributions have earned him numerous honors, including a knighthood from Queen Elizabeth II in 2004 and the prestigious ACM Turing Award often referred as the Nobel Price of computing, in 2016. He remains a vocal advocate for digital equality and user control over data.
